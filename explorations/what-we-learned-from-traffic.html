What We Learned from Traffic

We did not solve traffic by improving vehicles.

We solved it by structuring interaction.

Consider

When vehicles first appeared, movement scaled faster than coordination.

Dead End

Without shared structure:

collisions increased
confusion spread
responsibility blurred

Consider

The solution was not better drivers alone.

Consider

It was:

lanes
signals
rules
shared expectations

Direction

Infrastructure made interaction predictable.

Consider

Today, thinking systems are scaling in a similar way.

Dead End

But we lack:

shared structure
continuity of reasoning
visible intention

Conclusion

We may not need better systems alone.

We may need infrastructure for how reasoning moves.
